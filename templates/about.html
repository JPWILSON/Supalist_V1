Welcome to the world's tablebase

People find my explanations tedious. So I will use bullet points as an attempt for conciseness, in explaining why I made Supalist:

  • I often click only the first or second entry on a google results list - even though there are millions. This only takes me to the answer I need some of the time.
  • In 7 out of 10 searches I do, the answer I would prefer is a list of information, ordered in some manner. Its not what I get though, so the listing and ordering has to be done mentally. So, thats as far as Wikipedia gets me (unless the page has a list).
  • Unfortunately 'Buzzfeed-type' articles/youtube videos usually occupy the space where I require objective list results. (Eg. 10 best laptops of 2017).
  • I would like to order the lists myself on my own set of criteria (in the way you can do so on an SQL database. Eg. Show laptops for sale in Chicago under $800 with x processor and x graphics card that have more than 30 upvotes. Order by 1. Dist from Chicago, 2. Price increasing, 3. Upvotes, 4. Graphics card, etc
  • I think that lists are useful in millions of situations. Its how we analyze things in our heads. It removes subjectivity - I don't have to go through articles desrcibing the "best" or "coolest" or "greatest" things anymore. I can rank universities/ buildings/ countries/ temperatures/ anything! on criteria I am concerned about in this instance, I
  • OBJECTIVITY. We don't understand the world. Wikiped, Linkedi, Twit and FB all use explanations/ paragraphs. Lists should have Qunatitative data. Qualitative analysis should be sectioned separately, as our views and ideas grow and change.
  • I cannot make billions of lists... and Jimmy Wales coulgn't write millions of articles. That is, Supalist will work on the concept of User Generated Content.
  • In a world's first, I have combined the word "Table" with "Database" to form: Tablebase! Edgy.
